The Problem

Surgical cases fail for operational reasons, not clinical ones

Orthopedic and surgical centers performing implant-heavy procedures lose significant OR time every month to coordination failures that are entirely preventable. The problem is not clinical competence. It is a data and process problem that nobody with a technical background has properly solved.

01
Vendor rep confirmation gaps Verbal confirmations with no paper trail. Reps go silent. Nobody escalates until the morning of surgery.
02
Loaner tray sterilization failures Trays arrive too late for SPD to process. The case gets delayed or cancelled. It was preventable with 24 hours notice.
03
Implant size and availability gaps Preference cards are not verified against actual inventory. The right size is not on site. The surgeon finds out in the OR.
04
No single source of case readiness Coordination is split across texts, phone calls, and spreadsheets. Nobody has a live view of what is confirmed and what is not.
